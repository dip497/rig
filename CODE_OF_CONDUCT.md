# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

Rig is an inclusive community. We pledge to make participation in this
project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age,
body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics,
gender identity and expression, level of experience, education,
socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste,
colour, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Expectations

- Be kind, specific, and honest.
- Assume good faith. Default to curiosity before criticism.
- Focus on the code, the design, the idea — not the person.
- Acknowledge others' contributions and credit them.
- Accept constructive feedback without escalation; give it without
  condescension.
- Use welcoming and inclusive language.
- Respect disagreement; a reasoned "no" is fine. Persistent pressure
  is not.

## Unacceptable behaviour

- Harassment, insulting or derogatory comments, personal or political
  attacks.
- Public or private harassment, trolling, or deliberate intimidation.
- Sexualised language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of
  any kind.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, issues, or pull requests.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting.

## Enforcement

- Reports go to the project maintainer (`utsav.itsm@gmail.com`). All
  reports are reviewed and responded to, and confidentiality is honoured.
- Consequences scale with severity: warning → temporary ban → permanent
  ban. We try to be restorative rather than punitive where that's
  possible.
- Maintainers who violate this code are held to the same standard as
  anyone else, and repeated violations may result in loss of maintainer
  status.

## Scope

This Code applies within project spaces (repository issues, pull
requests, discussions, related chat rooms) and when an individual is
representing the project in public.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is adapted from the

version 2.1. It is intentionally short and pragmatic for an early
